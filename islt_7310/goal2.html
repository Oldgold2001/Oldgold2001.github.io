Landmark Cases

11 Landmark Casses

There are many court cases that are decided on every year. For the purposes of this website, we will focus on 11 Landmark US Supreme Court cases selected by the American Bar Association. Students will learn about the issue of the case and what caused it to be heard by the Supreme Court. This is often called the question of the case. The Students will learn about the basic facts of the case as well as the decision. Next the students will go to the oyez.com website to research the cases further. By looking at this website students will be able to see the majority, concurring, or dissenting opinion for each of the 11 cases.

American Bar Association Article

Court Cases

Case names will link to information on www.oyez.org/

  • Marbury v Madison (1803) - Established Judicial Review

  • McCulloch v Maryland (1819) – Established implied powers of Congress and federal supremacy

  • Gibbons v Ogden (1824) – Gave the federal government more power to regulate the economy

  • Dred Scott v Sanford (1857) – Stated Slaves were property and helped lead to Civil War

  • Schenck v U.S. (1919) – Set up the clear and present danger test for the freedom of speech in the U.S.

  • Brown v Board of Education (1954) – Overturned segregation laws in schools and laid the ground work for the Civil Rights movement of the 60s.

  • Gideon v Wainwright (1963) – Established the defendants right to a lawyer in all state criminal trials.

  • Miranda v Arizona (1966) – Said rights must be told to anyone being accused of a crime by the police. Created the Miranda Rights.

  • Tinker v Des Moines (1969) – Set parameters to First Amendment freedom of speech rights for students in schools.

  • Roe v Wade (1973) – Central case dealing with abortion rights in the U.S.

  • Regents of the University of California v Bakke (1978) – Important case that up affirmative action programs as constitutional.
